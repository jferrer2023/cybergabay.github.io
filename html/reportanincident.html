How to Report an Incident

Follow these step-by-step instructions to ensure your incident is properly reported and documented.

1

Ensure Safety First

Remove yourself from danger and ensure you and others are safe before reporting.

  • Move to a safe location if possible
  • Call 911 if immediate danger exists
  • Provide first aid if trained and safe to do so
  • Don't confront suspects or enter dangerous areas
2

Gather Information

Collect as much relevant information as possible while the details are fresh.

  • Date, time, and exact location of incident
  • Description of what happened
  • Names and contact info of witnesses
  • Physical descriptions of people involved
  • License plates, vehicle descriptions
  • Property damage or injuries sustained
3

Document Evidence

Take photos and videos when safe to do so, but prioritize your safety.

  • Photos of damage, injuries, or crime scenes
  • Video recordings if available
  • Screenshots of digital evidence
  • Preserve physical evidence
  • Don't disturb the scene unnecessarily
4

Contact Authorities

Report to the appropriate agency based on the type of incident.

  • Call 911 for emergencies
  • Use non-emergency lines for non-urgent matters
  • File online reports when available
  • Contact multiple agencies if needed
  • Ask for report numbers and case numbers
5

Cooperate with Investigation

Work with authorities and provide additional information as requested.

  • Be available for follow-up questions
  • Provide additional evidence if found
  • Attend court hearings if required
  • Keep all documentation organized
  • Stay in contact with investigating officers
6

Follow Up

Monitor the progress of your report and take additional action if needed.

  • Check status of investigation regularly
  • Contact supervisor if no response
  • File complaints if service is inadequate
  • Seek legal counsel if necessary
  • Document all interactions with authorities

⚠️ Important Reminders:

  • • Always call 911 for emergencies - Don't hesitate if someone's life or safety is at risk
  • • Document everything - Take photos, videos, and detailed notes when safe to do so
  • • Multiple reports are okay - It's better to report the same incident to multiple agencies than to miss reporting it
  • • Follow up - Keep record of report numbers and follow up on the status of your reports
