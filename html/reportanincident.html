How to Report an Incident

Follow these step-by-step instructions to ensure your incident is properly reported and documented.

1

Ensure Safety First

Protect yourself and your devices before taking action.

  • Disconnect from the internet if necessary
  • Avoid interacting further with suspicious messages or links
  • Do not share personal or financial details
2

Gather Information

Document details of the incident as accurately as possible.

  • Date, time, and platform where it occurred
  • Screenshots of messages, emails, or pop-ups
  • Sender’s address, username, or website links
  • Any suspicious attachments or downloads
3

Preserve Evidence

Secure all possible evidence for investigation.

  • Save emails, chat logs, and transaction records
  • Keep screenshots in a safe folder
  • Do not delete files or messages until reported
4

Report to Authorities

Notify the proper agencies or organizations.

  • Local police (if financial loss or threats are involved)
  • National cybercrime reporting center
  • Your school, workplace, or organization’s IT/security team
5

Notify Service Providers

Alert the platforms or companies involved.

  • Report phishing or hacking attempts to email providers
  • Inform your bank of suspicious financial activity
  • Use in-app reporting tools on social media
6

Follow Up & Stay Informed

Track your report and take preventive measures.

  • Get a reference or case number for follow-up
  • Monitor your accounts for unusual activity
  • Learn from the incident and strengthen your online security
